Subsets of Entities
A subset of an entity is a collection of occurrences of
an entity type that participate in relations or possess
attributes that are not common to the entity type as a
whole. The entity as a whole is called a superset in
this context.
• Subsets are nouns.
• Subsets can participate in relations.
• Subsets can have attributes.
For example, when you ask: ”Is this a relation between
Persons and Department?”, the user will reply: ”Yes,
but only the employees”

Dependent of Entities
A dependent of an entity is a noun that may have
attributes of its own but that exists only as part of,
or as subordinate to, some other entity. The entity
in this context is called the parent of the
dependent.
• Dependents are nouns.
• Dependents can participate in relations.
• Dependents can have attributes.

Dependent of Entities (cont.)
• Dependent must have parent.
• Dependent must have only one parent.
• Dependent must always have the same parent.
• The PK of a dependent is always the PK of its
parent(s) plus one additional column.

Recursive Relations
• A recursive relation is a relation between
occurrences of a particular entity type and other
occurrences of the same entity type. The entity
could be simple, subset or dependent entity.
• Recursive relations can be of the three basic types:
1-1, 1-M, M-N.
• Also, called Unary Relations.

Time Relations
A Time Relation is a relation between an
entity, a subset, a dependent, or another
relation and time.
• Time Relations are always many-to-many.
• Imagine a relation between Years, Months,
Days, Hours and Seconds.
• We only consider a virtual entity called
TIME.
